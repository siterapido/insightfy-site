The most common mistake when adopting AI agents is starting with the process that **looks best on a slide**, not the one operations can support. Result: endless pilot, zero ROI, skeptical team.

This guide is a filter. Use it in a 45-minute meeting with the people who live the process every day.

## Four axes

Score each candidate from **1 to 5** (5 = excellent for a first agent).

### 1. Volume and repetition

Are there tens or hundreds of similar cases per week? If every case is unique, automation costs more than it saves.

### 2. Rule clarity

Can you describe "if X then Y" for most cases? If every ticket becomes a philosophy debate, automate **after** you document the process — or only the mechanical slice.

### 3. Cost of error

What happens if the agent is wrong? A misclassified triage email is recoverable. An unsupervised tax posting is not. Prefer high volume and **low risk** in cycle one.

### 4. Available data and systems

Is there an API, export, inbox, or queue? Without the system of record, the agent is theater. Integration is half the work (sometimes more).

## Quick matrix

Sum the scores. Prioritize **16–20** with cost-of-error risk that is still acceptable.

| Process (example) | Vol. | Rules | Low risk* | Data | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 ticket triage | 5 | 4 | 4 | 5 | 18 |
| Weekly sales rollup | 4 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 17 |
| Contract negotiation | 2 | 2 | 1 | 3 | 8 |

\*Higher score = lower business risk if the agent fails.

## Anti-patterns

- Starting with a CEO-bot that "answers anything".
- Automating a process nobody documented.
- Picking a flow with 14 systems and no integration owner.
- Measuring success only by "wow in the demo".

## Define success in one sentence

Good examples:

- "Cut first-response time from 4h to under 5 minutes on 70% of L1 tickets."
- "Remove 80% of Monday-morning spreadsheet consolidation work."

Bad examples:

- "Improve support with AI."
- "Be more innovative."

## Pilot scope (2–6 weeks)

1. **One** process, **one** channel, **one** success metric.
2. Human in the loop from day one (approval or sample review).
3. Log every decision and action.
4. Weekly review: what the agent got right, escalated, or invented.

If metrics do not move in six weeks, the problem is rarely "the model". It is process, data, or scope.

## After the first win

Once one agent is in production with honest metrics, the second process is cheaper: you already have patterns for observability, handoff, and integration. Resist multiplying agents before that.
