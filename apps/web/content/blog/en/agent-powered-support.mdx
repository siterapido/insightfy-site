Support is the favorite demo use case for AI — and also where projects die when the agent "almost gets it right" and the customer feels coldness or factual error.

The right goal is not to **replace** the team. It is to **compress repetitive work** and keep humans on empathy, exceptions, and relationship.

## A flow that usually works

1. **Ingest** — ticket, email, or chat hits the queue.
2. **Classify** — intent, urgency, product, sentiment.
3. **Retrieve context** — order, contract, knowledge base, history.
4. **Draft a reply** or **take an action** (invoice copy, status, reset) within policy.
5. **Handoff** — if confidence is low, value is high, or policy requires it, a human takes over **with context already assembled**.
6. **Operational learning** — escalated cases improve KB and rules, not only blind fine-tuning.

## Where the agent truly saves time

- status questions and invoice copies
- questions covered by the knowledge base
- routing to the right queue
- summarizing history before a human opens the case

## Where humans stay in charge

- chargebacks and disputes
- enterprise / critical accounts
- complaints with reputational risk
- any irreversible action without confirmation

Practical rule: if the action **cannot be undone with one click**, the agent proposes; the human confirms — until accuracy and volume justify more autonomy.

## Metrics that matter

Ignore "bot satisfaction" as the only number. Track:

- **time to first response**
- **% resolved without a human** (with sampled quality)
- **reopen rate**
- **correct vs incorrect escalations**
- **CSAT/NPS on agent-touched cases** (vs baseline)

If response time drops and reopens rise, you only pushed the problem downstream.

```text
[INIT] ticket #4821 priority=high
[SCAN] intent=invoice_copy conf=0.91
[GEN] draft from KB + ERP invoice
[DONE] reply sent · 1.2s
[WARN] ticket #4822 intent=tax_dispute → human handoff
```

## Common traps

- Training only on tone of voice while ignoring ERP integration.
- Hiding the agent ("never say it is AI") instead of being transparent and useful.
- Enabling write access on day one without sample review.
- Measuring only headcount cost instead of support quality.
